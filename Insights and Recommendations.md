## 1. Product Performance Analysis

### Category Revenue Breakdown

| Category | Revenue | Orders | Quantity | Revenue % |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|
| Bikes | $106.1M | 18,368 | 90,268 | 86.1% |
| Components | $13.3M | 2,650 | 49,044 | 10.8% |
| Clothing | $2.4M | 9,877 | 73,670 | 1.9% |
| Accessories | $1.4M | 19,524 | 61,932 | 1.2% |

### Key Insights

**Product Concentration Risk**
- Bikes dominate revenue at 86.1% of total sales
- Top 10 products account for approximately $33.8M (27.4% of total revenue)
- All top 10 products are bikes, indicating heavy product concentration
- Mountain-200 Black 38 is the highest revenue generator at $4.9M

**Subcategory Performance**
- Road Bikes lead at $49.2M (46% of bike revenue)
- Mountain Bikes follow with $40.9M (39% of bike revenue)
- Touring Bikes contribute $16.1M (15% of bike revenue)

**Recommendations**

1. **Diversification Strategy**
   - Reduce dependency on bikes by developing premium clothing and accessories lines
   - Bundle accessories with bike purchases to increase category revenue
   - Introduce bike maintenance packages and services

2. **Inventory Optimization**
   - Focus stock on top-performing Mountain-200 and Road-250 series
   - Implement just-in-time inventory for low-margin accessories

3. **Product Development**
   - Invest in electric bike category given market trends
   - Create premium clothing line for serious cyclists

## 2. Sales Channel Analysis

### Online vs Offline Performance

| Channel | Revenue | Orders | Avg Order Value | Revenue % |
|---------|---------|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| Offline | $90.8M | 3,806 | $23,851 | 73.7% |
| Online | $32.4M | 27,659 | $1,173 | 26.3% |

### Key Insights

**Channel Characteristics**
- Offline orders have 20x higher average order value ($23,851 vs $1,173)
- Online channel generates 7x more orders but significantly lower value
- Offline likely represents B2B or dealer sales
- Online represents direct-to-consumer smaller purchases

**Recommendations**
1. **Online Channel Enhancement**
   - Increase average order value through cross-selling and upselling
   - Implement minimum free shipping thresholds to increase basket size
   - Create online-exclusive bundles and packages
   - Target: Increase online AOV to $2,000 (70% increase)

## 4. Time-Based Performance Analysis

### Year-over-Year Performance

| Year | Revenue | Orders | Quantity | YoY Growth |
|------|---------|--------|----------|------------|
| 2011 | $13.9M | 1,533 | 12,814 | - |
| 2012 | $35.1M | 3,765 | 63,402 | 152.2% |
| 2013 | $48.2M | 13,514 | 128,873 | 37.4% |
| 2014 | $26.1M | 12,653 | 69,825 | -45.9% |

### Key Insights

**Growth Trajectory**
- Strong growth in 2012 (152%) and 2013 (37%)
- Significant decline in 2014 (-46%) - concerning trend
- 2014 data appears incomplete (only through July based on revenue pattern)
- Peak performance in 2013 at $48.2M

**Seasonality Patterns**
- Strongest months: April, May, July (summer cycling season)
- Q3 consistently shows highest revenue
- December shows declining performance (winter season)
- Clear seasonal pattern with summer peaks

**Seasonal Planning**
   - Increase inventory and marketing investment for Q2-Q3
   - Develop winter product promotions and indoor cycling equipment
   - Create off-season maintenance and service offerings
   - Plan production and procurement around seasonal demand
